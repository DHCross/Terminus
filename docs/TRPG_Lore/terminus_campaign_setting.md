## Core Premise

**Terminus is a dark fantasy setting with high-fantasy remnants.**

The world is not chaotic by nature. It wants stability.

Routine stabilizes reality. Predictable markets, civic bells, transit lines, oath rituals, factory whistles, court procedures, ward inspections, burial customs, and repeated prayers all help hold the world in place.

When routine holds, ordinary life is possible. When routine fails, reality begins to rupture.

A Rupture may begin as something small: a bell ringing twice, a person repeating a step, a door opening into the wrong room, a street remembering an older city, a train arriving before it departed, or a clerk signing a document that has not yet been written.

The world does not break all at once. It loses permissions.

## The Lived World

The people of the world do not call it Terminus.

They call it **Tringad**.

Tringad is the lived world: the name found on maps, tax ledgers, transit schedules, charters, temple registers, court seals, family histories, and school primers.

Terminus is the buried architecture underneath it.

### Campaign Principle

Player-facing play should begin in Tringad, not in Terminus.

The secret can wait.

## Major Regions

### The Pinder Traverse

Central human civic nation. Roads, courts, summons offices, tolls, licenses, field contracts, and Accord administration.

### Skeld Holds

Stoneborn mountain nation in the north. Holdfasts, load-law, structural memory, bridge-priests, oath-stones, and deep vault courts.

### The Wild Verge

Wild Alfar homeland in the northeast. A shifting region of route-clans, seasonal courts, moving borders, and paths that may stop recognizing travelers.

### Morrow Fens

Deep Alfar region. Swamps, drowned roads, under-fen cities, hidden vaults, buried stations, and foundation records beneath visible maps.

### Black Ward Coast

Eastern warded coast. Human and High Alfar influence. Lighthouses, ward-families, storm protocols, dead harbors, old sea defenses, and coastal Ruptures.

### The Cereth March

Southeastern contested march. Militarized borderland, unstable jurisdiction, forts, smugglers, rival field cells, broken law, and disputed Accord authority.

### Iseth Basin

Archive-state and monumental record culture. Sun courts, tax-burial systems, legal afterlife, deep record vaults, and identity ledgers.

### The Deep Foundations

Southern sealed foundational ruins. Stoneborn and Deep Alfar significance. Dangerous, ancient, and closer to the hidden architecture than most surface places.

## Campaign Tone

Keep the setting grounded.

Use pressure, duty, procedure, memory, debt, and local consequence before cosmic explanation.

The setting should feel lived in:

- people have jobs, permits, routes, and obligations
- institutions can be competent, slow, corrupt, or exhausted
- danger is often bureaucratic before it is monstrous
- ordinary routines matter because they are holding the world together

---

## Hidden Truth

Terminus is the buried architecture beneath Tringad.

The campaign does not need to solve Terminus immediately.

Use the hidden truth as a source of:

- contradictions in civic memory
- impossible infrastructure
- repeated failures in old places
- records that disagree with themselves
- streets and systems that behave as if they remember another world

---

## Magic and Permission

Magic should feel formal, civic, dangerous, and often bound to authority.

Questions to answer per campaign:

- who is allowed to use magic
- what counts as legal or sanctioned magic
- what requires a seal, rite, permit, or witness
- what happens when magic is used without permission
